## Answer Key

*Scroll down only after you've answered all questions!*

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

**Q1:** A - World War I created a need for a new design aesthetic that could help society cope with the trauma of mechanized warfare.

**Q2:** B - Art Nouveau was characterized by its organic forms and nature-inspired designs, which were seen as outdated after the harsh realities of World War I.

**Q3:** B - Art Deco is known for its use of geometric shapes, bold lines, and a sense of luxury, contrasting the organic forms of Art Nouveau.

**Q4:** A - Egyptomania was a cultural obsession with ancient Egyptian art and motifs that significantly influenced the Art Deco style.

**Q5:** B - Nutzstil translates to 'useful style' and reflects the demand for a more functional and robust aesthetic in post-war design.

**Q6:** B - The step-back zoning laws required buildings to narrow as they rose, resembling the structure of Mayan temples.

**Q7:** C - The 1925 Paris Exposition aimed to assert France's leadership in modern design and introduced Art Deco to a wider audience.

**Q8:** B - Art Deco embraced the machine age by making industrial designs glamorous and appealing, thus helping society to reconcile with modernity.

**Q9:** B - Art Deco played a significant role in shaping modern consumerism by promoting luxury and aesthetic appeal in mass-produced items.
