**Q1:** B - The episode emphasizes how materials define eras and influence human development, moving away from traditional narratives focused on individuals.

**Q2:** B - Christian Jørgensen Thomson, a Danish museum curator, created these terms in the early 19th century as a way to organize historical artifacts.

**Q3:** A - The three-age system is Eurocentric and does not accurately represent the historical development of other regions, such as Sub-Saharan Africa and the Americas.

**Q4:** C - Bronze required tin, which was rare and had to be traded over long distances, making it expensive and a symbol of elite status.

**Q5:** B - Iron melts at a much higher temperature than copper or bronze, making it more difficult to work with using ancient technologies.

**Q6:** A - Alloying refers to the mixing of metals, specifically copper and tin, to create bronze, which has superior properties.

**Q7:** B - Iron tools enabled farmers to clear forests and cultivate previously unusable land, leading to increased agricultural productivity.

**Q8:** A - Ancient Egyptians, such as King Tutankhamun, used meteoritic iron, which is naturally occurring and didn't require smelting.

**Q9:** B - The episode highlights that the materials we master shape our perceptions and societal structures, indicating a deep connection between materials and psychology.
