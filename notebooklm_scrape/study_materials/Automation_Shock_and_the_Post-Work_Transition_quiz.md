**Q1:** B - The episode explores how societies might reorganize themselves in response to automation, focusing on the post-work transition.

**Q2:** B - The key takeaway is that we should focus on tasks rather than jobs, as automation impacts specific tasks within jobs.

**Q3:** B - The displacement effect refers to the loss of jobs due to automation, while the productivity effect refers to increased efficiency leading to potential job creation.

**Q4:** B - 'So-so automation' refers to technology that replaces jobs but does not lead to significant productivity gains or new job creation.

**Q5:** B - The episode discusses the Industrial Revolution, particularly the concept of Engels' pause, where economic growth did not translate to wage increases for workers.

**Q6:** B - This concept highlights the necessity for education systems to adapt and provide skills that match the demands of emerging technologies.

**Q7:** B - Polanyi's paradox states that we can know and do things without being able to explain them explicitly, which has historically protected certain jobs from automation.

**Q8:** B - The reinstatement effect suggests that when one task is automated, it can create opportunities for humans to engage in new tasks where they have a comparative advantage.

**Q9:** B - The term 'Prompt Engineer' is highlighted as a new job title that has emerged due to advancements in artificial intelligence and automation.
