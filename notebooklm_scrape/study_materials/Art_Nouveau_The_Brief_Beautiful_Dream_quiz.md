## Answer Key

*Scroll down only after you've answered all questions!*

<details>
<summary>Click to reveal answers</summary>

**Q1:** A - 'Art Nouveau' translates to 'new art', reflecting its aim to innovate and break away from historical styles.

**Q2:** B - The bourgeois class was newly wealthy and educated, seeking unique aesthetic experiences in their homes.

**Q3:** B - Art Nouveau rebelled against the academic art establishment's historicism and the devaluation of craftsmanship due to industrialization.

**Q4:** B - William Morris championed high-quality craftsmanship and influenced the aesthetic ideals of Art Nouveau.

**Q5:** B - Ukiyo-e prints introduced European artists to new graphic compositions and styles that significantly influenced Art Nouveau.

**Q6:** B - Art Nouveau is characterized by flowing, stylized, curvilinear forms that reflect natural growth.

**Q7:** A - The whiplash curve is a defining feature of Art Nouveau, symbolizing movement and organic forms.

**Q8:** B - Art Nouveau frequently featured highly stylized botanical and zoological motifs, reflecting a deep connection with nature.

**Q9:** C - Art Nouveau sought to reclaim the dignity of the artisan and elevate decorative arts as genuine works of art.
