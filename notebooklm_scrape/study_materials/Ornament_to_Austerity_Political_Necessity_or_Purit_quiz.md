**Q1:** B - The episode focuses on the transition from detailed ornamentation (like Rococo) to the severe austerity seen in styles such as neoclassicism and brutalism.

**Q2:** B - The rejection of Rococo was driven by Enlightenment criticisms of the centralized power and economic inequality represented by its aesthetics.

**Q3:** B - Rousseau criticized Rococo for being conceptually against nature and sought a return to pure, natural forms.

**Q4:** B - Neoclassicism aimed to visually construct a stable, masculine system of governance aligned with the new citizen class.

**Q5:** C - World War I led to a cynicism that affected artistic movements, as it demonstrated the destructive potential of technological efficiency.

**Q6:** B - Art Deco celebrated the use of industrial materials and geometric forms, whereas Art Nouveau focused on organic, nature-inspired designs.

**Q7:** C - Brutalism emphasized raw concrete and material honesty, reflecting a commitment to ethical architecture.

**Q8:** C - The podcast argues that aesthetic shifts often become revolutions when they are aligned with political ideologies and societal demands.

**Q9:** C - Aesthetic purity refers to the commitment to simplicity, rationality, and the rejection of superfluous ornamentation in design.
