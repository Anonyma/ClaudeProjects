# Vienna Notebook — Deep Dive Prompts for NotebookLM Audio Generation

## Instructions for Agent/User

Use these prompts in NotebookLM's "Customize" feature when generating Audio Overviews for the Vienna notebook. Each prompt is designed to generate a **long (60-minute) deep dive** on a specific topic.

**Settings:**
- Length: **Long** (or "Longer" if available)
- Expertise level: **Advanced**
- Use the "Customize" text box to paste the prompt

**Important:** Generate these one at a time, waiting for each to complete before starting the next. The Vienna notebook has 25+ sources but existing transcripts only cover Vienna Secession briefly - these prompts target completely uncovered territory.

## Prompt 1: Fin-de-Siècle Vienna - The Explosion That Changed Everything

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on Vienna around 1890–1914 — the "fin-de-siècle" period.

Focus on WHY this one city produced so many revolutionary thinkers simultaneously: Freud's psychoanalysis, Klimt and Schiele's radical art, Mahler and Schoenberg's musical revolution, the philosophical roots of logical positivism, Schnitzler's literary provocations, and the architecture of Otto Wagner and Adolf Loos.

Explore the cultural conditions that made this possible: the coffeehouse as intellectual laboratory, the role of Vienna's Jewish intelligentsia, the tension between the crumbling Habsburg empire and explosive creative modernism, and how bourgeois anxiety about the dying old order fueled radical new ideas.

Don't cover the Vienna Secession as an art movement — that's already been discussed in previous episodes. Instead focus on the broader intellectual ecosystem and why all these breakthroughs happened in ONE city at ONE time.

Expertise level: Advanced. Go deep.
```

## Prompt 2: The Habsburg Machine - How an Empire Shaped a City's DNA

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on the Habsburg dynasty and how their 600+ year rule shaped Vienna into the city it became.

Cover: How the Habsburgs used marriage instead of war ("Let others wage war; you, happy Austria, marry"), the strategic role of Vienna as a bulwark against the Ottoman Empire (the 1529 and 1683 sieges), how the Counter-Reformation turned Vienna into a Baroque showpiece, Maria Theresa's reforms and the golden age of the 18th century, the Congress of Vienna (1814-15) and how it made the city the diplomatic center of Europe, and Franz Joseph's 68-year reign during which the Ringstraße transformed Vienna from a medieval fortress into a modern capital.

Explore how Habsburg imperial culture created Vienna's DNA: the obsession with protocol, the coffeehouse, the waltz, the pastry, the specific Viennese character that persists today.

Expertise level: Advanced. Assume the listener has general European history knowledge.
```

## Prompt 3: Vienna's Sound - From Mozart to Schoenberg and the Death of Tonality

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on Vienna as the world capital of music.

Trace the arc from Haydn and Mozart establishing the Classical style in Vienna, through Beethoven's revolutionary expansion of the symphony, to Schubert's art songs, Brahms vs Wagner debates, the Strauss waltz dynasty, Mahler's massive late-Romantic symphonies, and finally Schoenberg's atonal revolution and the twelve-tone system.

Explore WHY Vienna specifically became the center of Western music: the role of aristocratic patronage, the competitive musical culture, how the coffeehouse functioned as a rehearsal/networking space, and why the break from tonality — one of the most radical moves in art history — happened HERE.

Connect to the broader fin-de-siècle anxiety: just as Freud was dismantling the rational self and Klimt was dismantling academic painting, Schoenberg was dismantling the harmonic system that had governed Western music for 300 years.

Expertise level: Advanced.
```

## Prompt 4: Red Vienna - The Socialist Utopia That Actually Got Built

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on "Red Vienna" — the period from 1918 to 1934 when Vienna's Social Democratic government created one of history's most ambitious municipal socialist experiments.

Cover: the massive public housing program (60,000+ apartments including the famous Karl-Marx-Hof), the worker education movement (Volksbildung), public health initiatives, kindergartens, public baths and pools, and how all this was financed by progressive luxury taxes.

Explore the architectural philosophy: why these housing blocks were designed as fortresses of the working class (and were literally used as fortresses during the 1934 civil war), how they contrasted with Le Corbusier's modernist housing concepts, and why Red Vienna's approach to social housing is being re-examined today as a model.

Connect this to the broader story of Vienna between the wars: the collapse of the empire, hyperinflation, the rise of Austro-fascism, and the Anschluss.

Expertise level: Advanced. Assume no prior coverage of this topic.
```

## Prompt 5: Adolf Loos vs. The World - Ornament and Crime

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on Adolf Loos and his radical 1908 essay "Ornament and Crime."

Explore: why Loos argued that ornament was literally criminal — wasteful of labor, materials, and human potential. His famous Looshaus on Michaelerplatz in Vienna (which horrified Emperor Franz Joseph), his concept of the Raumplan (spatial plan), and how his ideas anticipated and influenced the entire trajectory of modernist architecture.

Contrast Loos with his contemporaries in Vienna: the Secession artists he despised, Otto Wagner whom he respected but disagreed with, and Josef Hoffmann and the Wiener Werkstätte whose decorative arts he considered morally degenerate.

Explore the irony: Loos stripped buildings naked while personally living a life full of scandal. His ideas about ornament are often misquoted and simplified — what did he ACTUALLY argue?

Connect to the broader debate about whether the elimination of ornament was liberating or dehumanizing — a debate that's still alive today.

Expertise level: Expert.
```

## Prompt 6: Freud's Vienna - How One City Invented the Unconscious

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on Sigmund Freud and the intellectual world of Vienna that made psychoanalysis possible.

Cover: Why Vienna's specific cultural conditions — sexual repression under bourgeois morality, the role of the coffeehouse as confessional, the medical school tradition at the University of Vienna, the Jewish intellectual tradition of textual interpretation — created the perfect incubator for Freud's ideas.

Explore: Freud's key concepts (the unconscious, dream interpretation, the Oedipus complex, the death drive) and how they connected to what other Viennese thinkers were doing simultaneously — Schnitzler in literature, Klimt in art, Mahler in music. Schnitzler and Freud both explored sexuality and the unconscious but through different media.

Also cover the psychoanalytic schisms: Adler's split, Jung's break, and how Vienna became the battleground for competing theories of the human mind.

Don't just describe Freud's ideas — explain why they could only have emerged in THIS city at THIS time.

Expertise level: Advanced.
```

## Prompt 7: The Ringstraße - How Vienna Rebuilt Itself as a Statement

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on the Ringstraße — Vienna's grand boulevard built from the 1860s onward after Emperor Franz Joseph demolished the medieval city walls.

Explore: Why each building on the Ring was designed in a DIFFERENT historical style and what that choice meant — the Parliament in Greek (democracy), the City Hall in Gothic (civic freedom), the University in Renaissance (learning), the Opera in neo-Renaissance, the Burgtheater in Baroque. This was historicism as ideology.

Cover the social engineering aspect: the Ringstraße was designed to control public gatherings (wide boulevards prevent barricades), showcase imperial power, and provide the bourgeoisie with a stage for promenading.

Connect to the tension this created: the Secession movement was a DIRECT revolt against Ringstraße historicism. Adolf Loos mocked it. Karl Kraus satirized it. The very grandeur meant to project stability actually revealed the anxious artificiality of the Habsburg twilight.

Expertise level: Advanced.
```

## Prompt 8: Vienna 1938–1955 - Anschluss, War, and the Cold War City

```
Generate a long, in-depth deep dive on Vienna's darkest and strangest chapter: the Anschluss (1938), WWII, and the post-war occupation.

Cover: How Vienna's Jewish intellectual culture — which had powered the fin-de-siècle explosion — was systematically destroyed. The enthusiastic reception Hitler received in Vienna (the Heldenplatz speech), the deportation and murder of Vienna's Jewish population, and how the city's cultural identity was hollowed out.

Then cover post-war Vienna: the four-power occupation (American, British, French, Soviet sectors), the espionage capital of the Cold War (the "Third Man" era), the rebuilding of the State Opera as a symbol of cultural rebirth, and the 1955 Austrian State Treaty that restored independence.

Explore the uncomfortable question of Austrian memory: the "first victim" myth, the delayed reckoning with complicity, and how Vienna has (or hasn't) confronted this history.

Expertise level: Advanced. Be unflinching.
```
